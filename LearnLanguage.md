# Guide to Learning a New Language (Video Summary)

## Pick the Right Language

- Pick easy for beginners: Spanish, French, or Portuguese (easiest for English speakers, many official languages).
- Ensure right motivation (friends speak it, survival – not impress strangers, anime, school without passion).
- Use FSI chart for time (hard languages OK with motivation).


## Step 1: Learn Basics (1 Month)

- Google "beginner [language] textbook pdf" (free, library).
- Do practice exercises ( = 2 years Duolingo / 12 years school).
- Skip Duolingo/YouTube theory – do textbook.


## Step 2: Listening Practice

- Watch YouTube videos (interesting, understand 30%+).
- Add "easy" if fast/gibberish.
- TL captions OK (no English subs!).
- Context/repeated words → 70% comprehension.
- ReverseContext for words/phrases.


## Step 3: Speaking Practice

- Pause videos, repeat sentences out loud/whisper (pretend convo, imagine uses).
- Write useful sentences in notebook.
- Record self (talk day, questions; fill English gaps).
- Review recording, lookup (DeepL/ReverseContext).


## Step 4: Real Use

- Learn culture/politics (e.g., French protests).
- Talk to natives when traveling.


## Warnings

- Avoid human interaction if solo.
- English subs = no progress (e.g., anime with subs = Discord only).
- Wrong motivation = quit after 1 day.
